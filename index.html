THE STORY

Emerging Tech is a Playground

Don Allen Stevenson III is a creative technologist and futurist who treats emerging tech like a playground. He spent years at DreamWorks Animation training artists and developing tools, then shifted into AR, AI, and interactive media long before they became buzzy topics.

His work now sits at the edge of storytelling, real‑time animation, and digital culture — sometimes as an advisor to companies like Meta, Apple, Adobe, Google, and OpenAI, and sometimes as an independent artist making strange films, books, characters, or experiments that spread across Instagram and TikTok.

Teaching is a big part of his practice. He hosts live sessions, keynotes, workshops, and long‑form classes that help people understand the future without fear. He’s been featured in places like MIT Technology Review, Forbes, Wired, Business Insider, and Meta’s series with Keke Palmer. Underneath all the tech, though, he’s guided by family, curiosity, and the belief that better tools can make us more human, not less.

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of companies do you work with?
I primarily advise Fortune 500 companies, innovative startups, and creative agencies looking to integrate Generative AI and Spatial Computing into their workflows and products.

Engagements range from strategic advisory retainers to project-based creative direction. We usually start with a discovery session to identify high-impact opportunities for AI integration.
